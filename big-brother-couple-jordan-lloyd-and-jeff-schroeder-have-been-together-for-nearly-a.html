'Big Brother' couple Jordan Lloyd and Jeff Schroeder have been together for nearly a decade here

 Posted on August 13, 2024  |  4 minutes  |  672 words  |  Aldo Pusey

A showmance began between the two houseguests in 2009, when they met on the set of the CBS TV show. Now, after nearly a decade together, the longtime couple has two kids.

July 2009: Jordan and Jeff met on season 11 of "Big Brother"

ADVERTISEMENT

October 2009: Lloyd admitted that she had started dating Schroeder

"Right after the show, we'd only go about four days before seeing each other," the star told the publication about taking the next step in their relationship off-screen. "I went to Chicago to meet his family. He has a huge family."

February 2010: They competed on season 16 of "The Amazing Race"

ADVERTISEMENT

In 2010, the "Big Brother" sweethearts brought their love back to reality TV by competing on season 16 of "The Amazing Race." The couple placed seventh in the competition.

In an interview with TVGuide.com , Lloyd and Schroeder admitted that they bickered constantly during the stressful ordeal, but that the experience made them a stronger couple. Lloyd joked to the publication, "There were times I wanted to shoot him so bad and times where he wanted to strangle me, but we got through it."

July 2011: They returned to "Big Brother" as a team for season 13

ADVERTISEMENT

The powerhouse couple continued to ride the reality TV circuit in 2011 by heading back to "Big Brother" for the "dynamic duos" twist. Although Jeff was evicted earlier on, he won America's favorite houseguest once again and Jordan was able to make it to the final four.

July 2012: The web-exclusive series "Jeff and Jordan Do America" premiered

In the summer of 2012, the "Big Brother" alumni took their relationship cross-country on the online interactive CBS show, "Jeff and Jordan Do America." Fans were able to follow along as the couple made the trek from Chicago to Los Angeles.

September 2014: Jeff proposed to Jordan on the set of "Big Brother" season 14

ADVERTISEMENT

2015: The couple appeared on "Marriage Bootcamp: Reality Stars"

April 2016: Jordan announced that she was pregnant with their first child and that they were already married

After sharing a "road to the wedding" series on their joint YouTube channel, the couple surprised fans in April 2016 when they revealed that not only were they already married, but they were also expecting their first child together.

ADVERTISEMENT

"To be honest with you, we've been trying to wrap our heads around this," Schroeder said in a video titled "Our Big Surprise." "She's a couple of months along right now, so it's OK to tell everybody. Obviously, we're telling our best friends first you guys."

Although Schroeder and Lloyd had originally planned to wed in Mexico in September, they decided to tie the knot a few months early after finding out that they were expecting.

October 2016: The couple welcomed their son Lawson

Just a few months later on October 13, Schroeder shared the news that their son had been born, writing on Instagram "Welcome to the world Lawson Keith Schroeder."

November 2016: The couple opened up about their first few weeks as parents

ADVERTISEMENT

Shortly after welcoming Lawson, Schroeder and Lloyd sat down with Us Weekly and opened up about their first few weeks as parents, including sleepless nights, taking shifts, and pregnancy mood swings.

March 2018: The pair shared that they were expecting their second child

As per Us Weekly, on the series Schroeder anchors on YouTube, "Daily Blast Live ," the parents revealed that Lawson was about to become a big brother by having the toddler do the honors. Lloyd held up her son, who was wearing a T-shirt that read, "Soon to be a big brother," to the camera as her husband chimed in, "We're having a baby, but we don't know what we're having yet."

September 2018: Jordan gave birth to a baby boy

ADVERTISEMENT

On September 7, Schroeder took to Instagram to share the news with a collage of a couple and their newest addition at the hospital.

"Welcome to the world Layton Sarti Schroeder," he wrote.
