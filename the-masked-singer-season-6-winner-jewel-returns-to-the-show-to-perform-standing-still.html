The latest episode of The Masked Singer brought a wave of nostalgia and excitement to fans as Jewel, the celebrated winner of Season 6, graced the stage to perform her hit song “Standing Still.” The episode, aptly named “2000s Night,” was a blast from the past, and Jewel’s presence added an extra layer of charm and energy to the already electrifying atmosphere.

Jewel Returns To The Masked Singer To Perform Her Classic Hit

Jewel enthralled the audience with her incredible vocals and undeniable talent as she sang her 2001 hit while skillfully playing her blue guitar. Dressed in a blue metallic jacket, a jean skirt, and knee-high boots, Jewel effortlessly transported us back to the iconic fashion and music of the 2000s.

The audience and even the judges couldn’t resist joining in the singing, with Nicole Scherzinger and Ken Jeong happily participating from behind the panelists’ table. This special appearance by Jewel served as a reminder of her triumph in the previous season of The Masked Singer.

She emerged victorious, beating Todrick Hall’s Bull, and took home the coveted Golden Mask trophy. Since then, Jewel has continued to evolve as a musical icon, consistently impressing her fans with her talent and creativity.

Notably, Jewel released her thirteenth studio album, Freewheelin’ Woman, in April 2022, showcasing her enduring artistry and passion for music. Her experience on The Masked Singer certainly had a profound impact on her as an artist.

The Masked Singer 2000s Night Was Filled With Great Performances, Surprises

The 2000s Night episode featured a lineup of masked singers performing iconic hits from the 2000s in their quest to advance further in the competition. Among the featured contestants were Pickle, S’More, Gazelle, and Cow. The celebrity panelists also got into the spirit of the night, with Jenny McCarthy channeling her inner Britney Spears in an all-denim outfit.

Season 10 of The Masked Singer brought an exciting format change, dividing the celebrity singers into three groups and introducing special Battle Royale semi-final episodes. With 16 masked singers this season, the competition is stiffer than ever, with intriguing characters like Donut, Hibiscus, Anteater, and more keeping fans guessing.

The latest season has already delivered several surprise unmaskings, including Demi Lovato as Anonymouse, Anthony Anderson as Rubber Ducky, and Tom Sandoval as Diver. The mystery and excitement continue to build as fans eagerly tune in to watch the next unmasking.
